HCF/GCD Description: The Highest Common Factor (HCF) or Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of two numbers is the largest number that divides both numbers without leaving a remainder. Example: Input: a = 12, b = 18 Output: 6 Explanation: Factors of 12: {1,2,3,4,6,12}, Factors of 18: {1,2,3,6,9,18}. Common factors: {1,2,3,6}. The highest is 6. Hint: Use the Euclidean algorithm: GCD(a, b) = GCD(b, a % b)
